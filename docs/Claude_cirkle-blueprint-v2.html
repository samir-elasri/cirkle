Deployment model — manual rsync, not auto-deploy

Per requirement: Git is for version control only. Deployment to node14 happens only when explicitly run. Recommended pattern (script in scripts/deploy.sh):

  • 1. Pre-flight — ensure clean working tree, run tests, build assets (npm run build).
  • 2. Rsync over SSH — push app/ bootstrap/ config/ database/ lang/ resources/ routes/ public_html/ artisan composer.json composer.lock; exclude .env, storage/, vendor/, node_modules/, .git/.
  • 3. Remote post-deploy — over SSH, run composer install --no-dev --optimize-autoloader, php artisan migrate --force, php artisan view:clear && php artisan config:cache && php artisan route:cache.
  • 4. Smoke check — hit the homepage, verify HTTP 200.

Triggered with a single command: ./scripts/deploy.sh (or bash scripts/deploy.sh on Windows). No CI, no webhooks, no surprises.

01 What we're building

Project objectives

Cirkle is a directory that introduces clients to service providers, then steps out of the way. It does not process bookings or payments between the two parties — once a client reaches a supplier's email, the relationship is off-platform. Cirkle earns revenue from suppliers who pay to be listed and to unlock profile add-ons.

1. Four platforms, one site

The homepage opens in English as a landing page. The visitor must choose one of four platforms before anything else loads. The chosen platform is highlighted in yellow, and the catalogue of categories & professions is filtered to match it. Confirmed in the video: the four platforms differ in their categories and professions, not just language — a B2B mechanic and a residential mechanic are different listings with different competence sheets, and some professions exist in one platform only.

  • Residential — English · everyday consumers looking for a tradesperson or service
  • Residential — Français · same, French interface
  • B2B — English · businesses sourcing other businesses (different category & profession set)
  • B2B — Français · same, French interface

2. Membership & numbering

Every member gets a sequential ID shared across clients and suppliers: C02350, F02351, C02352… The number (without the letter) is shown top-right of the homepage as a live member counter (Denis: surface it once there are ~5–6k members). Critically: every supplier is automatically also created as a client, with a second, distinct number — so the two roles can be managed independently.

Cost landmine — confirmed in the video Denis insists on the auto-client-from-supplier rule purely to inflate his member count and give suppliers two entries in his draws. The previous agency flatly warned him it's conceptually broken (a supplier is a company, not a person — who is the "client"?) and said it "va te coûter une fortune." Scope it explicitly or carve it out of v1; don't let it silently bloat Milestone 1.

3. Supplier registration

Step 1 captures the legal basics — legal name, juridical form (travailleur autonome / société / association / coopérative / OBNL), federal tax number only (no corporation/business number, no driver's licence for suppliers), business address, city, postal code, business phone, fax, business email, business hours, business start date, owner names, and a password (with a show/hide eye toggle Denis asked for). Step 2 is the competence flow: pick Residential or B2B, then drill Category → Profession via the recursive list. A per-profession fee is then shown with an Accept gate.

Refined in the video The "Accept the fee" gate moves to the very top of the competence flow — right after platform → category → profession, before any of the form renders. Refuse → the rest stays hidden with a "you must accept to continue" message.

4. The supplier profile ("fiche")

Each supplier gets a profile page with tabbed navigation:

  • Profil — identity / contact, with a mailto "Contacter le fournisseur" button
  • Compétence — the default tab; the services checklist they filled in at registration
  • Conditional paid tabs — appear only if the supplier paid: Diplomas, Photos, Permits, Estimation, Recruitment. 5 in v1 — the Promotion tab is deferred (see Revenue).

5. The MASTER 2350 competence form — the heart of the site

Client's words "Toute la programmation de mon formulaire représente 90% de mon site." This one form is the core deliverable.

Confirmed against the file: 1_WEB_MASTER_2350 is one sheet (0001RF), ~1,324 rows. Column A = internal/programming labels (TITRE INTERNE "0001 RF 75/100/100", CATÉGORIE, PROFESSION, section markers) — never shown. Column B = the clickable "O" the supplier ticks to include a service. Column C = the service text shown. Column D = "X" flags. Only B & C are ever shown; unticked rows disappear from the public fiche. Denis fills these as Excel "fiches d'importation" that get imported; red markers vanish on save, blue text persists, and Excel spacing/indentation is rendered literally (he must trim leading spaces). The same template is cloned per profession (~200).

6. Evaluations & favourites — re-specced in the video

Evaluations were re-designed to work like Google reviews: only logged-in clients can rate (1–5 stars + a free-text comment); the fiche shows an average + count. Scores of 1–2 route to Cirkle (file 00_CLIENT_14F). The supplier can reply, but the reply needs admin approval before it publishes. New supporting pieces: a heart "favoris" system (clients manually favourite suppliers and professions, stored in their profile) and an "historique des consultations" — these replace the abandoned "contacted" tracking (see User flow).

7. Legal & privacy

Terms of Use / Privacy Policy to comply with Québec Law 25 — consent, access/rectification/deletion, breach notice to the CAI, automated-decision disclosure (file 00_FOURN_20F). The video adds two facts: the live Terms page is currently empty, and a paid cookie-consent tool (~$200/yr) is mandatory and is a separate external dependency the client must fund.

02 The core experience

The 3-click client journey

This is the single most important interaction on the site. Everything on the client side exists to serve it.

1
Enter postal code
Client types their postal code (or nearby). Available professions turn green, clickable, shown in columns with member numbers. The full black "liste récursive" (category-left, professions-right) is always present but never clickable.
2
Pick a profession
Clicking a green profession returns the list of member suppliers active in that sector — presented in random order for fairness.
3
Reach the supplier
Clicking "Contacter le fournisseur" opens a mailto to the supplier. The conversation is off-platform — Cirkle states it is no longer responsible past this point.
Dropped in the video "Fournisseur contacté" auto-tracking is removed — the site can't know if a client called by phone, so the data would be false. Replaced by the manual heart/favoris: a client clicks the heart on a supplier's fiche to save it, then reaches it later via profile → favoris (no need to re-search).
Still open — confirm before Milestone 1 How is "available in this postal code" decided? By the supplier's registered address, or a service-radius / the up-to-10 postal codes a supplier enters at registration? This single decision shapes the data model and search logic.
03 How the site makes money

Revenue model & paid add-ons

Three layers, all confirmed in the video: a one-time competence-fiche fee (default $75, but Denis wants it variable per profession — accepted at the top of the flow), a listing subscription / "website connection" in 1 / 3 / 6 / 12-month tiers (e.g. ~$100 / month up to ~$1,000 / year, plus per-postal-code and per-province options), and the optional add-ons below. Each add-on is a linked form: the supplier opens it, the price is shown, they accept → it's marked "Accepté" → redirected to payment → on success the tab unlocks. Add-ons are managed later from the profile (add / remove / renew). Promotion (PPROMOCK50) is deferred to post-launch by mutual agreement in the video — it's a large, custom chunk — so v1 ships with 5 add-ons. Its sub-options (3 photos $50 / 6 photos $80) come with it later.

Billing, per the video Stripe for cards (Interac via Stripe confirmed possible in Canada). Subscriptions are recurring: 7 days before expiry → automated email → pay or cancel in-profile; no action → auto-charge; card fails → 7-day grace to update. Cancellation takes effect only at term end, no refund. Invoices are emailed as a PDF + email body; a receipt follows payment (not an invoice before). All subscription/option management lives in the profile, not via email-to-support.
